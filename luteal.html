LUTEAL PHASE

Luteal is the last stage in the cycle. After releasing its egg, the follicle transforms into the corpus luteum.
This structure secretes hormones, primarily progesterone and some estrogen.
The increase in hormones keeps your uterine lining thick and ready for a fertilized egg to implant.
If you become pregnant, your body will create human chorionic gonadotropin. This is the hormone that pregnancy tests detect.
It supports the corpus luteum and keeps the uterine lining thick.If you do not become pregnant, the corpus luteum will shrink and be reabsorbed. This produces lower levels of estrogen and progesterone, resulting in the commencement of your menstruation. The uterine lining sheds throughout your menstruation.

NUTRITION

In this phase metabolism speeds up. Complex carbohydrates, proteins, and vitamins B6 and E are important. Foods that help regulate mood and reduce bloating are beneficial. It is also important to have magnesium in your diet.
